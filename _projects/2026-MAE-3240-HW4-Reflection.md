Reflection:

I chose this homework assignment as my most valuable and educational assignment from MAE 3240: Heat Transfer because it gave me the most insight on applying the theoretical heat transfer concepts we learn in lecture to real-life applications such as PV panels and composite materials. I gained more experience drawing thermal resistance networks, especially because this problem set involved a network with parallel resistances, which is not something we encountered in previous assignments. With this more thorough thermal resistance network, I learned to work with a heat transfer context involving all three modes of heat transfer in a composite system. Additionally, this assignment helped me analyze heat dissipation through extended surfaces like fins, which is not an area of heat transfer I have previously worked with. This type of problem with fins was not something I learned in a previous class of MAE 2210: Thermodynamics, so I am grateful that this assignment allowed me to grasp this new concept better.

Overall, this assignment involved a lot of new thermal topics and concepts for me, which allowed me to strengthen my analytical skills and conceptual understanding in heat transfer. This assignment improves my insight and skills in other classes I am taking related to thermal systems, such as MAE 4510: Propulsion or MAE 4860: Automotive Engineering. This assignment also ties into not only my courses but also extracurriculars and projects such as my research group and project team.
